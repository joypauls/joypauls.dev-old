## Setup

Let's look at a simple ordinary differential equation.

$$
\begin{cases}
\dot{\textbf{x}}(t) = \textbf{f}(\textbf{x}(t)), \quad \forall t > 0 \\
\textbf{x}(0) = x_{0}
\end{cases}
$$

where we are using the dot notation for derivatives: $$\dot{\textbf{x}}(t) = \frac{d}{dt} \textbf{x}(t)$$.

### Controls

What happens when we want our influence exerted over this system?

$$
\begin{cases}
\dot{\textbf{x}}(t) = \textbf{f}(\textbf{x}(t), \textbf{a}(t)), \quad \forall t > 0 \\
\textbf{x}(0) = x_{0}
\end{cases}
$$
